## Flexbox Was Necessary â€” But Not Sufficient

Flexbox gave us powerful control along **one axis at a time**.

- Navigation bars
- Toolbars

## Enter the Second Dimension

CSS Grid is a **two-dimensional layout system**.

You define:

- Columns
- Rows
- Gaps
- Structure

Then you place content _into_ that structure.

Not the other way around.

---

## The Mental Shift

Grid changes how you think.

You stop thinking in terms of:

- left / right
- top / bottom

And start thinking in terms of:

- **grid lines**
- **tracks**
- **areas**

You design the layout first.  
Then you assign content to it.

Thatâ€™s the second dimension.

---

## What Grid Makes Possible

With Grid, you can:

- Build full-page layouts cleanly
- Span elements across rows _and_ columns
- Rearrange layouts visually without touching HTML
- Redefine structure at breakpoints
- Layer and overlap elements intentionally

No floats.  
No hacks.  
No duct tape.
